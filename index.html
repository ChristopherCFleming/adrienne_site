A Lifetime of Public Service

Adrienne is a dedicated advocate for Cambridge families, equal access, and a brighter future. Her personal journey, rooted in social work, research, and public service, has shaped her vision for Cambridge. As a leader, Adrienne stands for inclusivity, affordable housing, and increased representation for marginalized communities. She envisions a Cambridge where resources are accessible to all, justice is equitable, and the environment is protected for future generations. By voting for Adrienne, you are voting for a candidate who will fight tirelessly to ensure a brighter future for families, promote social justice, and create a sustainable community. Join Adrienne in building a Cambridge that uplifts everyone.

Justice for All

Adrienne's research in Argentina shed light on the intersection of culture, health, and science, with a focus on access to justice for marginalized groups. She worked tirelessly to challenge problematic discrimination against female victims who were written off as “crazy.” Additionally, Adrienne spearheaded a focused deterrence program for lowering homicide rates. She then took these strategies to cities across the globe, emphasizing her dedication to creating safer communities.

A Better City
Starts with Family

Adrienne’s mother's deteriorating health compelled her to return to Massachusetts, where she has dedicated herself to supporting her while simultaneously engaging in public service. During this time, she married her Argentine husband, who has faced difficulties in transferring his certifications to the United States, hindering his professional growth. Together they navigated our complicated healthcare system, advocating for their son who underwent open-heart surgery at a tender age. These personal struggles have only fueled Adrienne's passion to ensure that Cambridge becomes a city that champions equal access to resources for all its residents.

Confronting
COVID

At the height of the pandemic, Adrienne's dedication to public service and disaster response training propelled her into action. As a key member of the NYC Medical Reserve Corps, Adrienne's exceptional leadership skills came to the forefront as she took leave from her remote job to volunteer for the Fatality Management Task Force, ensuring critical tasks were accomplished amid the chaos. When the already overburdened medical systems inevitably failed, she took a leadership position and forged a path forward.
